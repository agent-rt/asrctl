asrctl bench — dual-backend snapshot — 2026-05-02
====================================================
Hardware:  Apple M2 Pro, 32 GB
Build:     ReleaseFast static binary, 6.2 MB
Backends:  qwen3   = Qwen3-ASR-0.6B-Q8_0     (~1.5 GB on disk: model + mmproj)
           whisper = ggml-large-v3-turbo-q5_0 (~547 MB)

                          wall time
                          ─────────
EN short (5.4s audio)
  qwen3    cold/warm      ~1.6 s / ~1.2 s
  whisper  cold/warm      ~2.2 s / ~2.2 s

EN long (17s audio)
  qwen3    cold/warm      ~1.7 s
  whisper  cold/warm      ~2.1 s

ZH short (3.7s audio)
  qwen3    cold/warm      ~1.2 s
  whisper  cold/warm      ~2.2 s

ZH long (17s audio)
  qwen3    cold/warm      ~1.7 s
  whisper  cold/warm      ~2.2 s

Quality differences (sample EN long):
  ground-truth: "...The Qwen 3 ASR model is a 0.6 billion parameter
                  encoder-decoder architecture optimized for low latency
                  on Apple Silicon."
  qwen3:    "The Qwen-3 model is zero point six billion parameter
             encoder decoder architecture optimized for latency"
            ↑ dropped "ASR", spelled-out 0.6, "low latency" → "latency"
  whisper:  "The QEN 3ASR model is a 0.6 billion parameter encoder-decoder
             architecture optimized for low latency on Apple Silicon."
            ↑ literal phonetic spelling of "Qwen 3 ASR"

Quality differences (sample ZH long):
  ground-truth: "...让我们坐在长椅上..."
  qwen3:    "让我们坐在草椅上"   ← acoustic confusion 长 vs 草
  whisper:  "让我们坐在长椅上"   ← LM context recovers

Punctuation:
  qwen3:    full-width comma "，"
  whisper:  half-width comma ","

Conclusion:
  - qwen3 wins on raw speed (~30-50% faster) and Chinese punctuation style
  - whisper wins on LM-side context (catches obscure words via stronger prior)
  - both correctly transcribe basic samples; quality difference shows on
    less-common words and ambiguous phonetics

Reproduce:
  zig build -Doptimize=ReleaseFast
  bench/bench.sh
